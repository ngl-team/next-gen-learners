Lesson Plan Partner

I teach 6th grade ELA. I'm starting a unit on persuasive writing next week. Before you give me a lesson plan, ask me 5 questions about my students so you can personalize it.

Demo 2

Differentiation in 60 Seconds

Now adapt this for a student who understands the concepts but struggles with English as a second language. Keep the thinking demands high but simplify the language.
Now create a version for a student who finishes early and needs extension.

SAY: "60 seconds. Two differentiated versions. You made every decision. How many of your students know more than they can show you because of a language barrier? AI just removed that barrier. Not the rigor — the barrier."

Demo 3

Student Simulator

Act as a 7th grader who is confused about fractions. I'm going to explain it to you, and you tell me where my explanation is unclear or where a real student might get lost.

SAY: "Practice your lesson before class, not during it. Find the gaps before your students do."

Demo 4

Feedback Writer

Here's a paragraph a 5th grader wrote about their favorite animal: "Dogs are the best pets because they are fun and nice. They play with you and love you. I want a golden retriever." Give feedback the way a great teacher would — specific, encouraging, focused on one thing to improve. Don't rewrite it.

SAY: "Specific. Encouraging. One clear next step. And you decide if it sounds like YOU before it ever reaches the student. That's the partner mindset."
