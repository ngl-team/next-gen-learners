What Students Learn

Every Session, a New Skill Set.

Each session is standalone. Pick one, pick all of them, or add more. Students walk away with something tangible every time.

Session 1: What Is AI? (And What Isn't It?)

Students get hands-on with real AI tools. They see where AI gets things right, where it fails, and why that matters. They leave with a clear understanding of what AI actually is and how it works at a level they can explain to their parents.

  • Live demos with age-appropriate AI tools
  • Interactive exercises testing AI accuracy and limitations
  • Discussion: where AI shows up in their daily lives
  • Takeaway: Students can explain what AI is and isn't to anyone

Session 2: Create with AI

Students use AI as a creative partner. They generate stories, artwork, and ideas using age-appropriate AI tools. The focus is on prompting: how to talk to AI to get better results. The takeaway is that AI amplifies their creativity, not replaces it.

  • Hands-on creative projects with AI tools
  • Prompt engineering basics: how to communicate with AI
  • Students create something they take home
  • Takeaway: AI is a tool that makes their ideas bigger, not a replacement for thinking

Session 3: AI and Your Future

Students explore how AI is already showing up in school, careers, and everyday life. Real scenarios, real discussion. They walk away with a personal framework for when to use AI, when not to, and how to stay sharp as the technology evolves.

  • Real-world scenarios: when to use AI, when not to
  • AI in careers, school, and daily decisions
  • Critical thinking framework for responsible AI use
  • Takeaway: A personal AI toolkit and decision-making framework they keep
