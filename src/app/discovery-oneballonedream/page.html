Pain Point Analysis

What's Broken

Both markets rely on Google, word of mouth, and hope. But the severity is different.

Youth Soccer Pain Points
Severity: 5/5

Relocation Nightmare

Family moves, kid has zero way to find clubs. Google and hoping someone answers the phone. Steve confirmed: "People are moving all the time and yet they're relying on Google."

Severity: 4/5

No Mid-Season Discovery

Club needs a goalkeeper. No system to search for one. Word of mouth only. Steve: "There's no portal for clubs."

Severity: 4/5

Players Feel Trapped

Kids and parents don't know what else is out there. Clubs exploit this with false promises. No way to compare options.

Severity: 4/5

Information Asymmetry

Parents don't understand the difference between ECNL, ECRL, EDP. No tool to compare clubs, costs, or levels.

Severity: 3/5

Tryout-Only Entry

Most clubs only accept players at annual tryouts. Mid-season movement is ad hoc and random.

Severity: 3/5

Cost Opacity

Parents don't know what clubs cost until deep in the process. No way to compare fees across clubs.

Semi-Pro Pain Points
Severity: 5/5

No Portal Exists. Period.

Zero infrastructure. Players DM clubs on Instagram. Clubs post open tryouts on random websites. Nothing centralized.

Severity: 5/5

Careers On the Line

These are 17-23 year olds trying to go pro. Missing an opportunity isn't disappointing. It's career-ending. Every missed connection is a door that closes.

Severity: 5/5

Clubs Can't Scout

USL2 and NPSL clubs are small operations with tiny budgets. They can't afford scouts. They need players to come to them, but players have no way to find them.

Severity: 4/5

Seasonal Urgency

USL2 is a summer league (May-July). Rosters get built in spring. Narrow window. Miss it and you wait a full year.

Severity: 4/5

Geographic Fragmentation

130+ USL2 teams spread across the country. A player in CT has no idea what's available in Virginia, or even New Jersey.

Severity: 3/5

Eligibility Confusion

USL2 preserves NCAA eligibility. Many college players don't know this. Clubs need to communicate it but have no channel.

Discovery Framework

The Questions

Four groups. Each designed to pull out specific data. Don't read these word for word. Just steer the conversation naturally and listen.

Group 1
Talk to 5-10

Parents

Target: Parents of U14-U18 players at ECNL, MLS Next, or top travel level
Parents are the ones who pay. They're the ones who Google at midnight trying to find a club after a move. They're the ones who feel stuck when their kid is unhappy. If parents don't feel this pain, we don't have a product.
  1. "How did you find your kid's current club? What was that process like?"
  2. "If your family had to relocate tomorrow, how would you go about finding a new club?"
  3. "What's the most frustrating part about navigating the youth soccer system?"
  4. "Has your kid ever wanted to switch clubs? What happened? How did you figure out what else was out there?"
  5. "What information would you need to see about a club before you'd even reach out? Cost? Level? Coaching staff? Location?"
  6. "How much do you spend per year on your kid's soccer total? Club fees, travel, showcases, ID clinics, everything."
What This Tells Us

Whether parents actually feel the pain of discovery or whether they're fine with the current system. If 8 out of 10 parents say "finding a club was a nightmare," we have a product. If they say "it was fine, we just asked around," the pain isn't there for this group.

Group 2
Talk to 5-10

Coaches and Club Directors

Target: ECNL and MLS Next club directors first, then top travel/EDP directors
Clubs are the supply side. A platform with players but no clubs is useless. We need to know if clubs actually struggle to find players and if they'd use a tool to do it.
  1. "When you need to fill a roster spot mid-season, how do you find players right now?"
  2. "How many players or parents reach out to your club per month wanting to join? How do they find you?"
  3. "When someone emails you about their kid, what do you need to see before you'd bring them in for a trial? What's on your checklist?"
  4. "What's your biggest challenge when it comes to building rosters?"
  5. "If there was a free platform where you could search for available players by position, age, location, and level, would you use it?"
  6. "Would you list your club on a platform where relocating families or players looking for opportunities could find you?"
What This Tells Us

Whether clubs have a real problem finding players or whether word of mouth is good enough. If directors say "I wish I had a way to search for a goalkeeper in a 20 mile radius," we know exactly what to build. If they say "word of mouth works fine," the club side might not be where the pain is.

Group 3
Talk to 3-5

Youth Players (High School Age)

Target: Juniors, sophomores, freshmen at ECNL or MLS Next clubs
Players are the ones who feel trapped. They're the ones who don't know what's out there. If players say "I'd love to put myself out there for other clubs to see me," we have demand.
  1. "If you were unhappy at your current club, what would you do? How would you find a new one?"
  2. "Do you know what other clubs are in your area? How did you find out?"
  3. "Have you ever wanted to try out for a higher-level club like ECNL or MLS Next? How would you even go about that?"
  4. "If you could put up a profile with your highlights, stats, and position for any club in the country to see, would you do it?"
  5. "What would you want a coach to know about you before you show up to a trial?"
What This Tells Us

Whether players feel informed or in the dark about their options. If kids say "I'd put a profile up in a second," that's demand. If they say "I'm fine where I am," we know this group isn't the entry point.

Group 4
Talk to 2-3

Semi-Pro / Pre-Professional Players

Target: 18-23 year olds playing or trying to play USL2, NPSL, or USL Academy
This is the group with the sharpest pain. These are players trying to go pro. They need summer minutes in USL2, they're trying to get seen by USL Academy clubs, and there is literally nothing out there for them. Zero competition exists in this market.
  1. "How did you find your current team? What was the process?"
  2. "When you were looking for a USL2 or NPSL team, what tools did you use? Was there any platform or was it all word of mouth?"
  3. "How do you put yourself out there to be seen by clubs? Highlights on YouTube? DMs on Instagram? Emails?"
  4. "If there was a platform where you could create a profile and every USL2, NPSL, and USL Academy club could find you and reach out, would you use it?"
  5. "What's the hardest part about finding opportunities at this level?"
What This Tells Us

Whether the semi-pro market is as broken as the research suggests. If these players say "I literally DM clubs on Instagram and hope they respond," we've found a wide-open market with zero competition and real urgency. This is the group where a platform could genuinely change careers.

After Discovery

What Happens With the Data

Once all conversations are done, the data drives every decision from here.

  1. Pattern Recognition

    Every response gets compiled. We identify what keeps coming up across all four groups. If 8 out of 10 parents say "finding a club was impossible when we moved," that's a pattern. If 6 out of 10 directors say "I need a way to search for players," that's a pattern.

  2. Pain Ranking

    Which group has the most pain? Parents? Directors? Youth players? Semi-pro players? The group with the sharpest pain is who we build for first.

  3. Market Decision

    Based on the data, we decide: youth soccer, semi-pro, or both from day one. The conversations tell us the answer. Not assumptions.

  4. Feature Definition

    The things people say they need become the features we build. Not what we think looks cool. What they actually asked for. Profiles, search, matching, communication - shaped by real demand.

  5. Build

    Once we know who we're building for and what they need, the platform goes live in weeks. Not months. Weeks. The architecture is the same for both markets - profiles, search, matching, communication. The only difference is the user base and the league taxonomy.
