Discovery Phases
Active Now

Phase 1: Coaches and Parents

These are the people you're around every single day. You're at practice with coaches, you're talking to parents on the sideline. You don't need to schedule special meetings for this. Just steer the conversations you're already having and listen.

Phase 1A
Talk to 5-10 directors

Coaches and Club Directors

Target: ECNL and MLS Next club directors first, then top travel/EDP directors. If you can reach any USL2 or USL Academy coaches, even better.
You already have these conversations every day. You know what it's like to need a goalkeeper and have no way to find one beyond word of mouth. These are conversations you're built for. Just steer them a little and listen for the answers.
  1. "When you need to fill a roster spot mid-season, how do you find players right now?"
  2. "How many players or parents reach out to your club per month wanting to join? How do they find you?"
  3. "When someone emails you about their kid, what do you need to see before you'd bring them in for a trial? What's on your checklist?"
  4. "What's your biggest challenge when it comes to building rosters?"
  5. "If there was a free platform where you could search for available players by position, age, location, and level, would you use it?"
  6. "Would you list your club on a platform where relocating families or players looking for opportunities could find you?"
What This Tells Us

Whether clubs have a real problem finding players or whether word of mouth is good enough. This determines if the supply side of the platform has demand. It also tells us exactly what a player profile needs to include, because directors will tell us what they look at.

Phase 1B
Talk to 5-10 parents

Parents

Target: Parents of U14-U18 players at ECNL, MLS Next, or top travel level.
You see these parents every day at practice, at games, on the sideline. They're the ones who pay the fees, drive the kids, and make the decisions. If they don't feel the pain of finding clubs, the demand side of the platform isn't there.
  1. "How did you find your kid's current club? What was that process like?"
  2. "If your family had to relocate tomorrow, how would you go about finding a new club?"
  3. "What's the most frustrating part about navigating the youth soccer system?"
  4. "Has your kid ever wanted to switch clubs? What happened? How did you figure out what else was out there?"
  5. "What information would you need to see about a club before you'd even reach out? Cost? Level? Coaching staff? Location?"
  6. "How much do you spend per year on your kid's soccer total? Club fees, travel, showcases, ID clinics, everything."
What This Tells Us

Whether parents actually feel the pain of discovery or whether they're fine with the current system. Combined with coach data, we'll know both sides of the marketplace: do clubs need players AND do families need clubs? If both sides confirm the pain, we build.

Target: 10-20 conversations total (coaches + parents), then send Brayan the notes. Once Phase 1 data is in, Phase 2 unlocks.

Unlocks After Phase 1

Phase 2: Youth Players

Once we know what coaches want and what parents need, we talk to the players themselves. Do they feel trapped? Do they know what's out there? Would they actually create a profile?

Phase 2
Talk to 3-5 players

Youth Players (High School Age)

Target: Juniors, sophomores, freshmen at ECNL or MLS Next clubs.
Players are the ones who feel trapped. They don't know what's out there. If they say "I'd put a profile up in a second," we have demand from all three sides of the marketplace.
  1. "If you were unhappy at your current club, what would you do? How would you find a new one?"
  2. "Do you know what other clubs are in your area? How did you find out?"
  3. "Have you ever wanted to try out for a higher-level club like ECNL or MLS Next? How would you even go about that?"
  4. "If you could put up a profile with your highlights, stats, and position for any club in the country to see, would you do it?"
  5. "What would you want a coach to know about you before you show up to a trial?"
What This Tells Us

Whether players feel informed or in the dark about their options. If kids say "I'd put a profile up in a second," that's demand from the user side. Combined with Phase 1, we'll have validated all three sides: clubs, parents, and players.

Unlocks After Phase 2

Phase 3: Semi-Pro Players

The market with zero competition and the sharpest pain. These are 18-23 year olds trying to go pro with literally no tools. AC Connecticut's USL2 team gives you a direct line into this world.

Phase 3
Talk to 2-3 players

Semi-Pro / Pre-Professional Players

Target: 18-23 year olds playing or trying to play USL2, NPSL, or USL Academy.
This is the group with the sharpest pain. No portal exists. They DM clubs on Instagram and hope for a response. If this group validates, it could be the market we attack first because there is zero competition. You have a direct line to these players through AC Connecticut's USL2 team.
  1. "How did you find your current team? What was the process?"
  2. "When you were looking for a USL2 or NPSL team, what tools did you use? Was there any platform or was it all word of mouth?"
  3. "How do you put yourself out there to be seen by clubs? Highlights on YouTube? DMs on Instagram? Emails?"
  4. "If there was a platform where you could create a profile and every USL2, NPSL, and USL Academy club could find you and reach out, would you use it?"
  5. "What's the hardest part about finding opportunities at this level?"
What This Tells Us

Whether the semi-pro market is as broken as the research suggests. If these players say "I literally DM clubs on Instagram and hope they respond," we've confirmed a wide-open market. This data combined with everything from Phases 1 and 2 gives us the complete picture to decide: youth, semi-pro, or both.
